PulseStrum

Stop Counting. Feel the Beat.

GuitarRhythm TraineriOS & Android

Your metronome has been doing half the job. It clicks — you do the rest: translating one flat beep into down-up-mute-rest, in your head, every single time. That's not rhythm training, that's mental math with a guitar in your lap.

PulseStrum does the other half. It shows and plays your actual strum pattern — a visual pulse sweeping across the grid, synced to a sound that changes with every step: an accented click on the downbeat, a softer click on the offbeats, and silence exactly where your hand should mute or rest. You're not decoding a click anymore. You're watching and hearing the groove itself, in real time.

Why This Works

A plain metronome trains your ear to count. PulseStrum trains your hand to anticipate — because the pulse and the sound tell you what's coming next, not just when. Practice long enough with a click and you're still translating it every time you sit down. Practice with PulseStrum and the pattern starts to feel automatic, because you've been seeing and hearing the real motion all along, not a proxy for it.

How It Works

Pick a pattern, set your tempo, and hit play. The pulse sweeps across the grid in sync with the sound — down, up, mute, or rest — so your eyes, ears, and hand are all learning the same rhythm at once.

Built-In Patterns

Start with proven strumming patterns across styles — pop, rock, folk, ballad, and more — or unlock genre-specific grooves like Reggae Skank, Bo Diddley Clave, Motown Split, and Bluegrass Drive.

Double Count Mode

Expand to a 16-step grid to train the "and" of every beat — the foundation for syncopation and real up/down independence.

Custom Chord Progressions

Add your own chords to any pattern and practice syncing your strumming hand with real chord changes, not just a click track.

Save Your Grooves

Build a personal library of custom patterns and reload them instantly for every practice session.

Make It Yours

Choose from multiple color themes, including a bright light mode, to match your practice space.
